About
This map allows you to view active mobile food vending commissaries in New York City.
  • An active commissary is open for business according to NYC Health Department information.
  • Click on a red dot to see the details of the commissary including name, address, and phone number.
  • Only those commissaries that have a permit issued by the NYC Health Department are included on this map.

Commissaries must obtain a non-retail food processing establishment permit.

How To Use This Map
Use this interactive map to see areas where active mobile food vending commissaries are located as well as their names, phone numbers and permit information.

Basic Search
To get started, enter a street address, ZIP code or neighborhood in the search bar. You can also zoom to your current location by using the My Location button.

Use your cursor and zoom buttons to move around the map.

Selecting a Commissary
Red dots on the map indicate active commissary locations. Click on a red dot to see a pop-up box with more information.

Commissaries in New York City
Food vending units must be stored at a commissary or depot. Green Carts and units that sell only pre-packaged foods may use an alternative facility approved by the NYC Health Department.

A food vending unit must be cleaned and serviced at least once every day. This must be done at a commissary or a Health Department-approved alternative facility.

Learn more about requirements to clean, service, and store your food cart or truck.

Food must be prepared using the commissary's food preparation facilities. At your pre-permit inspection you must provide your agreement with a commissary or request for an alternate approved facility. Visit nyc.gov/health/mobilefood for the forms.

Below is a list of active commissaries. If you would like a free copy of the commissary’s most recent inspection report, contact the Mobile Food Vending Inspection Program at 212-676-1600/50 or email infoBFSCS@health.nyc.gov.
